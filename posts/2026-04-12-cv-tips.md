Your CV is not a life story. It is a sales document — and it has exactly one job: to get you into the room for an interview. Most South African job seekers do not understand this, and it costs them real opportunities every single week.

The truth is that recruiters at South African companies spend between 7 and 10 seconds scanning a CV before deciding whether to read further. Your document needs to work inside that window.

## Keep it to two pages — maximum

A one-page CV is acceptable for someone with less than three years of experience. For everyone else, two pages is the sweet spot. More than two pages signals that you do not know how to prioritise information — and that is not a trait that gets you hired.

## Start with a strong professional summary

The top section of your CV should be three to four sentences that tell the recruiter who you are, what you have done, and what you bring. Not your "objectives." Not what you are "looking for." What you *offer*.

**Example:** "Office administrator with six years of experience in HR benefits processing and payroll administration at a major South African bank. Proven track record in high-volume telephonic advisory, compliance record-keeping, and employee data management. Seeking to bring structured, detail-oriented administration to a professional services environment."

Short, specific, and immediately useful.

## Match the language in the job posting

South African employers — and their automated screening tools — look for keyword matches. If the job posting says "data capture and filing," your CV should use those exact words, not "document management." Read the posting three times before you submit.

## List achievements, not just duties

Most CVs read like job descriptions. Yours should read like a performance review.

Instead of:
> *Responsible for processing payroll adjustments*

Write:
> *Processed payroll adjustments for 800+ employees monthly with a 99.8% accuracy rate*

If you do not know your numbers, estimate conservatively. "Handled high-volume telephonic queries, averaging 60+ calls per day" is far more powerful than "answered calls."

## Format for ATS and humans

Most medium-to-large South African employers use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) before a human ever sees your CV. That means:

- Use a clean, single-column format
- Avoid text boxes, tables, and columns
- Use standard headings: Work Experience, Education, Skills, References
- Save as PDF unless the posting specifically requests Word format

## References

"References available on request" is completely acceptable. You do not need to list names and numbers on your CV — it wastes space that could carry stronger content.

## The one rule that matters

Read your CV out loud. If any sentence is awkward, vague, or could describe anyone — rewrite it. Your CV should sound like you at your most professional, not like a template you downloaded years ago.
