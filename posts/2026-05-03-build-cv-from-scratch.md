## Why Your CV Is Your Most Important Career Document

Your CV is often the only thing standing between you and an interview invitation. A South African recruiter handling 200 applications for a single role will spend, on average, less than 30 seconds on each CV in the first pass.

That means your CV must communicate your value clearly, quickly, and professionally. A badly formatted, poorly organised CV will be rejected before anyone reads its content.

This guide walks you through every section, what to include, what to leave out, and how to present it.

## Format and Layout First

Before you write a word, get the format right.

- **Length:** Two pages maximum for candidates with up to ten years of experience. Three pages for senior candidates. One page if you're a recent school leaver or have minimal experience, and that's fine.
- **Font:** Calibri, Arial, or Cambria. Size 11 for body text, size 12-14 for headings.
- **Margins:** Standard (2.5 cm on all sides). Don't shrink margins to cram in content.
- **Colour:** Use one accent colour sparingly (dark blue or charcoal grey are professional). Avoid bright colours, images, or elaborate design for corporate roles.
- **File format:** Save as PDF unless the employer specifies Word.
- **File name:** Your name and the word "CV", e.g., *Sipho_Dlamini_CV.pdf*. Not "CV Final Version 3 UPDATED."

## Section 1: Contact Details

At the very top. Clear and complete.

- Full name (in a larger font, this is your heading)
- Phone number (the one you answer)
- Professional email address (e.g., siphodlamini@gmail.com, not playboyking99@yahoo.com)
- City and province (you don't need your full street address)
- LinkedIn profile URL (if your profile is complete and professional)

**Don't include:** your ID number, date of birth, marital status, or a photo (these aren't required in South Africa and including them can expose you to discrimination risks and isn't standard practice in professional CVs).

## Section 2: Professional Summary or Objective

Two to four sentences. This is your pitch, who you're, what you bring, and what you're looking for.

**If you've experience:**
> *Results-driven HR administrator with six years of experience managing end-to-end recruitment, employee records, and benefits administration in corporate environments. Known for accuracy, discretion, and the ability to manage multiple competing priorities. Seeking to bring my skills and passion for people management to a forward-thinking organisation.*

**If you're a school leaver or entry-level:**
> *Dedicated and motivated recent matriculant with strong communication skills and a genuine interest in customer service. Completed a customer service learnership at XYZ and eager to apply practical skills in a professional environment.*

Keep it focused. No generic buzzwords like "team player" or "hardworking" unless backed by something specific.

## Section 3: Work Experience

List in **reverse chronological order**, most recent role first.

For each role, include:
- **Job Title** (in bold)
- **Company Name** and **Location**
- **Dates of employment** (Month Year - Month Year, or "Present")
- **Bullet points** describing your responsibilities and achievements

**Writing the bullets:** Start each with an action verb. Be specific. Quantify where you can.

*Example:*
**Customer Service Representative** | Nedbank | Johannesburg | Jan 2022 - Mar 2024
- Handled 80+ inbound calls daily, resolving product and account queries within SLA targets
- Achieved a 94% customer satisfaction score consistently over 18 months
- Trained three new team members on CRM systems and call handling protocols
- Escalated complex complaints to senior consultants, reducing average resolution time by 25%

**If you've gaps in employment:** Don't try to hide them with vague dates. Be honest. A gap followed by a voluntary course, freelance work, or personal circumstances explained briefly in a cover letter isn't disqualifying, dishonesty about it's.

## Section 4: Education

List in reverse chronological order, most recent first.

Include:
- Institution name
- Qualification obtained
- Year completed (or expected graduation year)
- Relevant subjects or distinctions if they strengthen your application

*Example:*
**National Certificate: Business Administration (NQF 4)** | MANCOSA | 2023
**National Senior Certificate** | Soweto Comprehensive Secondary School | 2019

If you've a degree or diploma, you don't need to list matric, unless the job specifically requires it and you want to confirm you've it.

## Section 5: Skills

A concise list of relevant, demonstrable skills. Don't list soft skills like "communication" or "teamwork" in this section, those belong in your summary and your bullets.

This section is for:
- Software: Microsoft Office (specify which packages), SAP, Salesforce, Pastel Payroll, etc.
- Languages: *English (fluent), Zulu (home language), Sotho (conversational)*
- Certifications: First Aid Level 1, FAIS regulatory exams, CIPS, etc.
- Technical skills relevant to the industry

## Section 6: Certifications and Training

If you've completed short courses, learnerships, or professional certifications, list them here:

- Name of certification / course
- Issuing institution
- Year obtained

This section is especially important for candidates who are building their careers through short courses and learnerships rather than formal degrees.

## Section 7: References

At the bottom, simply write: **"References available on request."**

Don't list references directly on your CV. This takes up valuable space and exposes your references to unsolicited contact. Have a separate reference document ready, with two or three professional references (former managers or supervisors, not friends or family), and provide it when requested.

## Final Checks Before You Send

- Read it aloud, awkward sentences become obvious
- Check every spelling and grammar issue twice
- Ensure all phone numbers and email addresses are correct
- Confirm the file saves cleanly as a PDF
- Ask one other person to read it with fresh eyes

Your CV is a living document. Update it every time you change roles, complete a course, or gain a new skill. Don't wait until you desperately need it to begin rebuilding it from scratch.
