## The Platform Landscape in South Africa

South Africa has a mature online job market. The major platforms each serve slightly different segments:

### PNet (pnet.co.za)
The largest and most widely used job board in South Africa. Strong across banking, finance, insurance, retail management, IT, and professional services. Most major corporate employers advertise here. If you are applying for corporate or professional roles, you must be on PNet.

### LinkedIn
The global professional network is well-established among South African corporate recruiters and headhunters. LinkedIn is particularly powerful for mid-to-senior level roles, contract work, and being approached by recruiters without actively applying. Profile quality matters enormously here.

### Indeed SA (za.indeed.com)
High volume, broad coverage. Particularly good for entry-level, operations, retail, and blue-collar roles. Many smaller businesses advertise exclusively on Indeed because it is free for employers. Apply here if you want volume and range.

### CareerJunction (careerjunction.co.za)
Strong for professional and technical roles in corporate environments. Often used by large staffing agencies alongside PNet.

### Careers24 (careers24.com)
Broad coverage with strong representation from staffing agencies. Good for temp and contract placements. Also strong in hospitality, manufacturing, and healthcare.

### DPSA (dpsa.gov.za)
The Department of Public Service and Administration vacancy circular is the authoritative source for all national and provincial government positions. Published weekly. If you want a government job, this is non-negotiable.

### LinkedIn Jobs vs LinkedIn Network
These are different things. LinkedIn Jobs is the formal application board. The LinkedIn network — connections, messages, content — is where the real power lies. Many South African corporate roles are filled through LinkedIn outreach before they are even posted.

## How to Optimise Your Profiles

### Keywords Are Everything
Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan for keywords from the job description. If your profile and CV do not contain the words the employer is searching for, you will not appear in results — regardless of how suitable you are.

Study job descriptions for roles you want. Note the repeated phrases, skills, and qualifications. Make sure these appear naturally in your profile and CV.

### Use a Professional Photo (LinkedIn and PNet)
Profiles with photos receive significantly more views. On LinkedIn, a professional headshot (not a casual selfie, not a group photo) is essential. Clean background, professional attire, natural smile.

### Complete Your Profile to 100%
On PNet and LinkedIn, an incomplete profile signals low engagement. Fill in every section. Most platforms show a profile completeness indicator — get it to 100%.

### Keep Your Availability Status Current
Most platforms allow you to indicate whether you are actively looking, open to opportunities, or not looking. Keep this accurate. "Actively seeking" profiles receive more recruiter contact.

## Setting Up Effective Job Alerts

Do not just browse daily — let the platforms come to you. Set up job alerts with specific:

- **Job title keywords** (e.g., "administrator," "customer service," "compliance officer")
- **Location** (city, or "remote" if applicable)
- **Salary range** if the platform supports it
- **Industry** filter

Review alerts daily when you are actively searching. Respond quickly — competitive roles in South Africa can receive hundreds of applications in the first 24 hours.

## Applying Strategically, Not Massively

The temptation on job platforms is to click "apply" on anything that looks remotely related to your experience. Resist it.

Each application to a relevant role with a tailored CV and cover letter is worth twenty generic spray-and-pray applications. Recruiters see the effort — and the lack of it.

Before applying to any role, ask:
- Do I meet at least 70–80% of the requirements?
- Do I actually want this job and could I see myself doing it well?
- Is my CV relevant enough to get through the first filter?

If the answer is yes, apply properly. If not, pass.

## Direct Employer Applications

Many South African employers — especially large corporates, banks, and retailers — have their own careers portals. Nedbank, Standard Bank, Absa, Woolworths, Pick n Pay, Capitec, and most large employers actively recruit through their own websites.

These applications often skip the general job boards entirely. Roles posted directly on a company's website may have fewer applicants than those on PNet, giving you a better chance.

Bookmark the careers pages of every employer you want to work for and check them weekly.

## Following Up After Applying Online

Most online applications disappear into a system without acknowledgment. If you have not heard back within two weeks and have a contact name or HR email, a brief, professional follow-up email is appropriate:

> *"Good day, I submitted my application for the [Role] via [Platform] on [Date] and wanted to confirm receipt. I remain very interested in the opportunity."*

Do not follow up by calling the switchboard repeatedly. One polite written follow-up is enough.

## The Platform Is the Tool — The Network Is the Advantage

The most successful job seekers use platforms as one component of a broader strategy. Platforms give you reach and volume. But the connections you build — former managers, colleagues, recruiters — give you access to the roles that never get posted.

Use both. The platforms are where everyone is looking. The network is where fewer people are — and the opportunities are often better.
